# Criteria

## Requirements:

* 2 pages
* 1.0 space
* minimum 2cm margin
* 11pt or 12pt font size
* PDF

### Submission Details

<mark style="color:blue;">**Page 1:**</mark>

* Personal Information
  * Student name and login
  * Supervisor name
  * MSc programme
* Full reference of the paper you have reviewed

<mark style="color:blue;">**Page 2 and 3:**</mark>

* The critical review

## Structure

### Summary (1 page)

A brief summary of the work in your own words; demonstrate the understanding of the paper

#### <mark style="color:blue;">**Motivation:**</mark>

* what is the motivation of the research work?
* is there a crisis in the research field that the paper attempts to resolve?
* is the research attempting to overcome the weakness of existing approaches?
* is an existing research paradigm challenged?
  * _<mark style="color:blue;">**what is the niche of the paper?**</mark>_

#### <mark style="color:blue;">**Contribution**</mark>

* what is new in this paper?
* is a new question asked?&#x20;
* a new understanding of the research problem?
* a new methodology for solving problems?
* new algorithms?
* new breed of software tools or systems?
* new experimental method?
  * _<mark style="color:blue;">**what is innovative about this paper?**</mark>_

#### <mark style="color:blue;">**Methodology and/or Argument**</mark>

* what is the methodology adopted to substantiate the claims?
* what is the argument of the paper?
* what are the major theorems?
* what experiments are conducted?
* case studies?
  * _<mark style="color:blue;">**what makes the claims scientific?**</mark>_

#### <mark style="color:blue;">**Conclusion**</mark>

* what have we learned from the paper?
* shall the standard practice of the field be changed as a result of the new findings?
* is the result generalizable?
* can the result be applied to other areas of the field?
* what are the open problems?
  * _<mark style="color:blue;">**what are the lessons one can learn from the paper?**</mark>_

### Critique (½ page)

Two /three points that would be interesting to argue with the authors of the paper.

Give technical reasons to substantiate your critiques.

#### <mark style="color:blue;">Importance</mark>

* is the research problem significant?
* is the work scratching minor itches?
* are the authors solving artificial problems?
* does the work enable practical applications, deepen understanding or explore new design space?

#### <mark style="color:blue;">Significance</mark>

* is the paper worth reading?
* are the authors simply repeating the state of art?
* are there real surprises?
* are the authors aware of the relation of their work to existing literature?
* is the paper addressing a well-known open problem?

#### <mark style="color:blue;">Validity</mark>

* have the authors been cutting corners?
* has the right theorem been proven?
* errors in proofs?
* problematic setup?
* confounding factors?
* unrealistic artificial benchmarks?

### Synthesis (½ page)

One or two ways in which the research work can be further developed.

* what is the crux of the research problem?
* what are some alternative ways to substantiate the claim of the authors?
* what is a good argument against the case made by the authors?
* can the research results be strengthened&#x20;
* what are the open problems raised by this work?
* _<mark style="color:blue;">**If you were to do the research, how would you do it differently?**</mark>_
