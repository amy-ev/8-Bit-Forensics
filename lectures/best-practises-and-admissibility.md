#### ACPO Guidelines

{% tabs %}
{% tab title="Principle 1" %}
No action taken by law enforcement agencies, persons employed within those agencies or their agents should change data which may subsequently be relied upon in court

_<mark style="color:blue;">the integrity of the evidence needs to be preserved</mark>_
{% endtab %}

{% tab title="Principle 2" %}
In circumstances where a person finds it necessary to access original data, that person must be competent to do so and be able to give evidence explaining the relevance and the implications of their actions.

_<mark style="color:blue;">Forensically competent; able to demonstrate, explain and justify implications of actions</mark>_
{% endtab %}

{% tab title="Principle 3" %}
An audit trail or other record of all processes applied to digital evidence should be created and preserved. An indepenedent third party should be able to examine those processes and achieve the same results.

Contemporaneous Notes. _<mark style="color:blue;">Repeatable results; record of processes with evidential value.</mark>_
{% endtab %}

{% tab title="Principle 4" %}
The person in charge of all the investigation has overall responsibility for ensuring that the law and these principles are adhered to.
{% endtab %}
{% endtabs %}

### <mark style="color:blue;">Reliability</mark>

* Whether the system source of the digital evidence (or the process used) <mark style="color:blue;">produces accurate results.</mark>



### <mark style="color:blue;">Authenticity</mark>

* satisfy the court that...&#x20;
  * the digital evidence was <mark style="color:blue;">acquired from a specific system and/or location</mark>
  * <mark style="color:blue;">a complete and accurate copy</mark> of the digital evidence was acquired, and
  * the digital evidence <mark style="color:blue;">remained unchanged</mark> since it was collected.

<mark style="color:blue;">**Chain of Custody**</mark>

* Controlling access to physical evidence; <mark style="color:blue;">who, when & why</mark>

<mark style="color:blue;">**Proof of Integrity**</mark>

*   Cryptographic hashing

    * the hash value (digest) of any evidence used during the investigation has to be documented.



### <mark style="color:blue;">Hearsay</mark>

Evidence might not be admitted in court if it contains <mark style="color:blue;">**hearsay.**</mark>

* where a statement in court repeats a statement made out of court to prove the truth of the out of court statement.
* Digital evidence <mark style="color:blue;">created by humans</mark> must comply with hearsay rules
* Digital evidence <mark style="color:blue;">generated by algorithms</mark> is _not_ subject to hearsay rules
  * machine-generated evidence, the issue is _authenticity and reliability._



### <mark style="color:blue;">Best Evidence</mark>

The <mark style="color:blue;">**best available evidence**</mark> should be provided to court

*   courts may accept <mark style="color:blue;">identical duplicates</mark> of an original evidence, unless a genuine question is raised about...

    * the authenticity of the original _or_
    * the accuracy of the copy _or_
    * circumstances where it would be unfair to admit the copy in place of the original.



### <mark style="color:blue;">Scientific Evidence</mark>

Admissibility of the <mark style="color:blue;">**tools and methods**</mark> used in investigations can be challenged

* if a <mark style="color:blue;">scientific process</mark> is found to be questionable, the admissibility and weight of the evidence may be influenced.&#x20;
*   <mark style="color:blue;">Daubert standard</mark> (for novel tools)

    * Whether the theory or technique in question can be (and has been) <mark style="color:blue;">tested</mark>
      * Whether the theory or technique has been subjected to <mark style="color:blue;">peer review and publication</mark>
      * Whether there is a high known or potential error rate, and the existence and maintenance of <mark style="color:blue;">standards controlling</mark> the technique's operation
      * Whether the technique has <mark style="color:blue;">'widespread acceptance'</mark> within the relevant scientific community&#x20;



### <mark style="color:blue;">Authorised search</mark>

Evidence will not be admitted in court if it has been obtained <mark style="color:blue;">**without (legal) authorisation**</mark>

* search warrants need to demonstrated to a magistrate that there are r<mark style="color:blue;">easonable grounds to believe</mark> that...
  * a crime has been committed _and_
  * evidence of that offence will be found on the premises being searched
